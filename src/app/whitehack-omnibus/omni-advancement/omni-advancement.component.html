Worn helmets shatter to negate rolling on this table. Crits always do double damage, stacking with the deft's double damage. Missile Attacks Weapon ranges are the close range.
  • The attack gets −1 AV for each exceeded increment of the close range value, up to a maximum of −4 (longer shots are impossible).
  • When firing into a melee, you must beat the AC of your target by 4 or more in order to hit it. If you beat it by less, you hit someone else in the melee, at the Referee's discretion. A miss is still a miss, though.
  • Targetting a certain spot is a task roll. Hitting a moving target's spot is a missile attack vs AC 0. Targets in area get to save to avoid damage. The area of effect 15×15 feet, or 9 grid squares. Can be arranged into a cone for breath weapons, or spread differently in a tight space. For ray weapons, count the number of squares crossed in the line of fire (9 squares = 45ft).
Special Combat Options
  • Charge. Charge 15 feet in a straight line to attack with +2AV & dmg. You get −3 AC until your next turn.
  • Grapple. Make a regular unarmed attack, but forsake your damage to hold your opponent. The opponent must win a contest against your strength to break free.
  • Protect. Make a task roll against your AV (i.e. not an attack). If you succeed, the next attack on an adjacent friend targets you instead. Enemies may save to avoid it.
  • Ready. Use your action to prepare a course of action, ex: “when the enemy strikes my ally, I want to cast a spell” or “when the enemy enters melee range, I want to strike them”. This consumes your free attack.
  • Trick. Attack and exchange your damage roll for one of the following effects, each negated by an enemy save:
    • Push the enemy five feet backwards or diagonally backwards, and follow into her former space if you like.
    • Feint to make two attacks with +2AV & dmg. in the next round.
    • Disarm the enemy so that her weapon drops 2d6 feet away.
    • Trip the enemy so that she loses one move in the next round, giving you +2AV & dmg..
  • Press. Take a penalty to your AV when using a special combat option. Give your opponent the same penalty to her save. If you fail or if the save succeeds, the enemy gets +2AV & dmg. against you in the next round.
Healing & Injuries A character who has not gone below zero HP will restore all hit points with 48 hours of rest.
  • Recovers 1 HP every evening and morning, 2 HP if she succeeds at a constitution task roll.
  • A character can bind 1d6−3 HP worth of wounds immediately after a battle, with a trained task roll. Fumbling = -1HP. Can only bind HP lost in that battle.

If your HP went into the negatives, you are injured. Heal at usual rate, but only to HP 1. Also counts as carrying five regular items.
  • To remove injured condition, must be treated by someone with a healer vocation—a physician, a healer, an old crone, etc.
  • Can remove the injured condition quickly by giving the character a permanent disadvantage, noted next to a trait. The disadvantage can be used to demand negative double rolls or to nullify the positive double roll benefit of a group for a particular task.
  • When someone uses a weapon’s knock-out special ability, the resulting damage can’t kill but only knock out. This means that you heal normally even from negative HP.

 Boss Battle
When a boss battle starts, all PC can choose to regain a used up class ability, or free up a slot, or recover 2d6 HP.
If the boss reduces your HP < 0, save vs death to stay at 1HP. If you are hit by the boss at HP < 0 you must save vs death:
  • Pass. Recieve one permanent injury
  • Fail. Die instantly
If you recieve 3 permanent injuries during the course of a boss fight, you die. Likewise if any damage would reduce you to -10 HP, you die.
